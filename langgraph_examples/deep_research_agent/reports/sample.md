## 1. Primary Causes of Facial Acne and Relation to Skin Types

Facial acne is primarily caused by excess sebum production, clogged hair follicles, and the buildup of dead skin
cells [1][2]. These factors can lead to the formation of pimples, blackheads, whiteheads, or more severe forms of
acne [6]. The relationship between facial acne and different skin types is complex, as various skin types have unique
characteristics that may influence acne development. For instance, individuals with oily skin are more prone to
comedonal acne due to the buildup of dead skin cells and sebum within hair follicles [7]. On the other hand, those with
dry or sensitive skin may experience inflammatory acne, which requires products that target bacteria and
inflammation [4]. Understanding the underlying causes of acne and its relation to different skin types is crucial for
effective treatment and management. According to recent studies, staying hydrated can help reduce excess oil production,
which can lead to acne breakouts [4]. Furthermore, research suggests that acne can appear almost anywhere on the body,
particularly in areas with numerous sebaceous glands, such as the face, back, chest, shoulders, and buttocks [3].
Effective treatment plans should consider the location of breakouts, skin type, and underlying causes of acne to provide
personalized care. For example, treatment plans for T-zone acne may include oil-reducing products and deep cleaning
pores regularly, as well as exfoliating to remove dead skin cells before they can clog pores [4]. In conclusion,
understanding the primary causes of facial acne and its relation to different skin types is essential for developing
effective treatment strategies. By considering the unique characteristics of each skin type and the underlying causes of
acne, individuals can take a more personalized approach to managing their acne and achieving clearer, healthier skin.

## 3. Ideal Daily Skincare Routine for Acne-Prone Skin

For individuals with acne-prone skin, establishing an effective daily skincare routine is crucial to prevent breakouts
and address existing acne [1][2]. According to dermatologists, a basic skincare routine should include three essential

further [4]. The ideal daily skincare routine starts with cleansing twice a day, using a cleanser that is suitable for
acne-prone skin, such as those containing salicylic acid or benzoyl peroxide [5][6]. Following cleansing, applying a
toner can help unclog pores and prevent breakouts, especially if it contains ingredients like salicylic acid or
AHAs [7]. Moisturizing is also critical, even for oily skin, as it helps to hydrate the skin without clogging pores;
look for lightweight, oil-free moisturizers with SPF for daytime use [8][9]. For nighttime, a moisturizer rich in
hyaluronic acid, lipids, and proteins can provide deeper hydration [10]. Exfoliating once or twice a week can help
remove dead skin cells and prevent clogged pores [11], while spot treatments containing ingredients like salicylic acid
or benzoyl peroxide can be applied as needed to individual pimples [12]. Sunscreen is a must, even on cloudy days, to
protect the skin from UV rays that can exacerbate acne [13]. The order of product application matters: cleanser, toner (
if using), treatment products (like spot treatments or exfoliants), moisturizer, and finally sunscreen [14][15]. By
following this routine and choosing products wisely, individuals with acne-prone skin can help prevent breakouts, reduce
inflammation, and achieve clearer, healthier-looking skin.
